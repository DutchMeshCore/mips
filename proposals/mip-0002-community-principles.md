## Summary

Define shared principles that guide future mesh proposals and decisions.

## Motivation

A proposal process works better when participants share baseline principles for interoperability, legal awareness, and practical operations.

## Proposal

The mesh community adopts these principles:

1. Openness: decisions should be discussed publicly by default.
2. Interoperability: avoid unnecessary fragmentation between regions.
3. Legal and safety awareness: proposals must consider regulatory and operational risk.
4. Regional flexibility: local constraints are valid and should be documented.
5. Practicality: prefer solutions that volunteers can operate reliably.
6. Accountability: decision rationale is written and preserved.

## Impact

These principles act as review criteria for future MIPs and help resolve ambiguous tradeoffs.

## Alternatives

- No explicit principles: leads to inconsistent evaluations.
- Highly prescriptive constitution: too heavy for current scale.

## Rollout

Use these principles immediately as non-binding guidance in proposal review and decision summaries.

## Compatibility

No software impact. Documentation and governance only.
